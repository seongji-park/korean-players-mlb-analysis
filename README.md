# Korean Players in MLB — A Pathway Analysis

A study of Korean players' development trajectories in Major League Baseball, 
segmented by outcome tier and analyzed within era-specific contexts. The work 
aims to inform development frameworks for the next generation of Korean amateur 
signings, with particular focus on the Toronto Blue Jays' first Korean-born 
international signing, Seojun Moon.

## Project Status

Work in progress — Currently in data collection phase

## Research Framework

This analysis examines nine Korean players who entered MLB through international 
amateur signings, organized into four tiers by outcome:

- **Tier 1 — Success Cases:** Park Chan-ho, Kim Byung-hyun, Choo Shin-soo
- **Tier 2 — Mid Cases:** Bae Ji-hwan, Choi Ji-man, Bong Joong-keun
- **Tier 3 — Raw Prospects:** Cho Won-bin, Kim Seong-jun
- **Tier 4 — Newest Entry:** Moon Seo-jun

The analytical structure is built around three layers:

1. **Pathway Segmentation** — Distinguishing KBO veterans from international 
amateur signings to avoid common comparison errors
2. **Era-Adjusted Context** — Mapping each player's success factors to the 
MLB environment of their era
3. **Forward Application** — Translating historical patterns into 
contextual implications for current prospects

## Methodology

- **Data sources:** MLB Statcast (via pybaseball), Baseball Reference, 
KBO records, and minor league development data
- **Analysis tools:** Python (pandas, numpy, scikit-learn), 
visualization via matplotlib and seaborn
- **Era-context framework:** Cross-referencing each player's MLB tenure 
against league-wide pitching, hitting, and roster construction trends 
of the period
